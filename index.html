Getting Started

TriPlan is a triathlon training planner built for athletes who want a clean, visual way to plan their swim, bike, and run training. Here's how to get going:

1
Log in — Enter the admin password to access the app. This is a shared gate for all users.
2
Choose your profile — If you're new, click "New User" and fill in your name, birthday, password, and target races. If you're returning, select your profile and enter your password for editing access, or browse in view-only mode.
3
Start planning — You'll land on the Calendar view showing the current week. Click the + button on any day to add a workout.

Using the Calendar

The calendar displays your training week in a grid layout with one column per day. Each week shows a summary bar at the bottom with total run miles, bike miles, swim yards, and session count.

Navigation

Use the arrow buttons in the nav bar to move between weeks. The single arrows ← → move one week at a time, while the double arrows ⟪ ⟫ jump four weeks. Click Today to snap back to the current week.

Week Start Day

By default, weeks run Sunday–Saturday. You can change this to Monday–Sunday in your Profile settings or from the Analytics tab's "Week Starts" dropdown. This preference is saved to your profile.

Today's date is highlighted with an orange accent in the day header so you can quickly find where you are.

Adding Activities

Click the + button on any day to open the activity modal. You'll see:

🏃
Run — tracked in miles
🚴
Bike — tracked in miles
🏊
Swim — tracked in yards

Select the activity type, enter the distance, and optionally add notes describing the workout (e.g., "Tempo run", "200 FS, 200 Pull, 200 FS", "Z2 easy spin"). Click Save or press Enter to add it.

To remove an activity, hover over it (or tap on mobile) and click the × button that appears in the top-right corner of the card.

Drag & Drop

Need to move a workout to a different day? On desktop, click and hold any activity card, then drag it to the target day. The destination will highlight with an orange dashed border. Release to drop.

Note: Drag & drop works on desktop browsers only and requires edit access. On mobile, delete and re-add using a template (it's fast — see below).

Activity Templates

When you open the Add Activity modal, you'll see a Quick Templates section at the top. This shows your recent unique workouts as clickable chips. Tap one to instantly fill in the type, distance, and notes.

To save a workout as a permanent template, check the "Save as template for quick reuse" checkbox before saving. Saved templates always appear first in the list.

Templates are great for recurring workouts — your standard swim set, your regular Z2 ride, your go-to tempo run.

Planned vs Actual

The Planned / Actual toggle at the top of the app switches between two independent data layers:

📋
Planned — your training plan. What you intend to do. Add workouts here when planning your week ahead of time.
✅
Actual — what you actually completed. Add workouts here after you've done them, or sync from Strava to auto-import.

Actual activities display with a dashed border and a small "✓ actual" tag so you can visually distinguish them. The Analytics tab has a Planned vs Actual chart that compares the two side-by-side.

Weekly Goals

Click the 🎯 Goals button on any week header to set target volumes for that week. You can set separate goals for run (miles), bike (miles), and swim (yards).

The weekly summary bar will show your progress against each goal:

✓
Green — goal met or exceeded
○
Red — goal not yet reached

Athlete Profiles

TriPlan supports up to 5 athlete profiles. Each profile has:

Name, birthday, and age (auto-calculated)
A personal password for edit access
A/B/C target races with dates, distances, and countdown timers
Independent planned and actual activity data
Week start day preference (Sunday or Monday)
Optional Strava connection

Click the Profile button in the header to view your full profile, change your password (requires birthday confirmation), adjust your week start day, or manage your Strava connection.

Use the athlete tabs in the header to switch between profiles. You can always view any profile, but you can only edit the one you logged into with the correct password.

Analytics Dashboard

The Analytics tab provides six visualizations of your training data. Use the controls at the top to adjust the time range, data source, compare with another athlete, and set the week start day.

📊 Weekly Volume

Bar chart showing your volume per week for a single discipline. Use the Run / Bike / Swim tabs to switch between them. If you've selected a comparison athlete, their data appears as a second bar in each week. This is your go-to chart for spotting training load trends — are you building consistently, or are there gaps?

📊 Monthly Volume

Stacked bar chart combining all three disciplines per month. Run and bike are in miles; swim is displayed in hundreds of yards (so "25" means 2,500 yards). This gives you the big-picture view of how your total training volume shifts month to month.

🍩 Discipline Split

Donut chart showing the percentage breakdown of your training across run, bike, and swim. The volumes are normalized (swim yards are converted to equivalent effort miles) so the proportions are meaningful. Useful for checking if you're neglecting a discipline.

📈 Cumulative Mileage

Line chart tracking your season-to-date totals for run and bike over the selected time range. The filled area shows the accumulation over time. If you've selected a comparison athlete, their run total appears as a dashed line overlay. Great for seeing if you're on track for a mileage target.

📊 Planned vs Actual

Side-by-side bar chart comparing your planned volume against your actual completed volume per week. All three disciplines are combined into "equivalent miles" (swim yards normalized). This reveals how well you're executing your plan — are you consistently hitting your targets, or falling short?

🟧 Training Heatmap

A GitHub-contribution-style grid where each cell is one day. Darker orange = more training volume that day. All disciplines are combined (swim normalized). Hover over any cell to see the exact date and volume. This makes rest days and training blocks visually obvious at a glance.

Strava Integration

You can connect your Strava account to automatically import completed activities into your Actual data. This requires one-time setup:

1
Register a Strava API app at strava.com/settings/api
2
Deploy a small Cloudflare Worker (free) to handle the secure token exchange
3
Add your Client ID and Worker URL to js/strava.js

Once configured, click Connect Strava in your Profile modal. After authorizing, click ↻ Sync Recent Activities to import your last 30 days of runs, rides, and swims. The sync is smart — it won't duplicate activities that have already been imported.

Strava imports Run, Bike, and Swim activities. Other activity types (hiking, yoga, etc.) are skipped. Distances are automatically converted: meters → miles for run/bike, meters → yards for swim.

Data & Sync

All training data is stored in Firebase Firestore (cloud database). This means your data syncs automatically across all your devices — laptop, phone, tablet. No manual export/import needed.

Export

Click the Export button in the header to download a JSON backup of all athlete data. This is useful for archiving or if you ever want to migrate to a different system.

Each athlete's data is independent — editing one profile never affects another. The admin password gates entry to the app, while individual user passwords control edit access to each profile.
